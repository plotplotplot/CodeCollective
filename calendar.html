Saturday, June 28, 2025 at 01:00 PM EST

@Wonderground Park

Tech Unity Cookout

How pleasant it is when the people of tech come together in unity!

In the case of inclement weather, the event will occur virtually

After a year of talking about it, we’re finally doing it: a chill summer cookout for everyone in Baltimore tech. No booths, no buzzwords — just food, sun, and a mic for group leaders to explain their mission.

We are hoping to cultivate a virtuous environment of Baltimore's best tech leaders, to share organizational tips, and to lean into Smalltimore's unique social strengths in building the future economy. Ultimately, we hope to foster the relationships that create better lives for ourselves and our people.

Agenda

  1. Networking Time (Breakout groups if virtual)
  2. The Official Ceremony - An hour and a half of brief talks from speakers representing each corner of the Baltimore Tech Economy
  3. Networking Time (Breakout groups if virtual)

The official ceremony will proceed something like this:

Julian Coy (Code Collective) - Keynote - the Baltimore Tech Economy Judy Johnson (DevOps Columbia) - DevOps is for Everyone Khalif Cooper (BLK Tech Connect) - On Underserved Communities Cornelius Hairston (Baltimore Tech) - How to Organize Tech Events Tony Li (Indie Game Devs) - How Gaming Drives Tech Forward Matt Peters (CryptoMondays) - Happenings in Crypto Forest Rawly (Baltimore Node) - On Makerspaces Eliot Pearson (Digital Harbor Foundation) - The 12 Nonprofits of Baltimore Tech

Free admission. Food will be provided (if in-person)!

Weather-permitting, the event will occur at Wonderground Park in collaboration with the Greenmount West Community Association. It's nearby Guilford Hall Brewery, home of EquiTech Tuesday and occasional host of Baltimore Rhythm Festival.

Sunday, June 08, 2025 at 04:00 PM EST

@Online event

The Economy - Open Discussion [VIRTUAL]

It's the economy, stupid!

This is the place to discuss the BIG picture of the modern tech economy. Anything goes! California Capitalism, Military Keynesianism, the United States Dollar, Bitcoin and Etherium, Unemployment, Automation, and what we can do in the Baltimore area to bring prosperity to our people.

Code Collective organizer Julian Coy will be briefly introducing his proposals for the Baltimore AI Project and the Baltimore Dirtbike Project, programs designed to make use of available facilities to build a strong local economy with less dependence on foreign nations and the federal government.

Do you have special economic understanding or a similar BIG project in mind? We would love to hear it in this 2-hour virtual moderated discussion. RSVP for link.

Sunday, May 04, 2025 at 01:00 PM EST

@Reveille Grounds

Industrial 3D Modeling with OpenSCAD

Sign up for STEM Rebellion here

Greetings, Esteemed Members of The Rebellion,

Come learn the basics of 3D modeling with OpenSCAD — a powerful, free design tool where you create models using simple code! In this beginner-friendly workshop, we’ll guide you step-by-step as you write your name in 3D using OpenSCAD, preparing it for 3D printing.

Bring your Windows or Linux laptop!

We’ll help you get set up, write your first OpenSCAD script, and customize your design. By the end of the session, you’ll have a 3D model of your name, ready to print and take home a piece of your creativity! No prior coding or 3D design experience needed — just bring yourself, your laptop, and your curiosity.

🛠️ What You'll Learn:

  • Installing and running OpenSCAD (Windows & Linux)
  • Writing simple OpenSCAD code
  • Making 3D text models
  • Saving your work for 3D printing

May the Fourth be with you!
