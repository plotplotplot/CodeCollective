# Structural Logic

The following is a professional sequence a licensed structural engineer would typically follow to detail the rowhome stair framing, roof opening, guards, waterproofing coordination, fire separation, and load paths.

1. **Establish Code Basis**
   Confirm governing codes: IBC/IRC, local amendments, ASCE 7, NDS/AISC/ACI as applicable, fire-resistance requirements, guard/stair geometry rules, roof live loads, snow loads, wind uplift, seismic category, and any rowhome-specific party-wall requirements.

2. **Review Architectural Intent**
   Review plans, sections, stair layout, roof access intent, parapets, roof deck/garden loads, waterproofing build-up, ceiling heights, and the exact stair opening geometry. Confirm that the stair clears the roof opening and required headroom.

3. **Survey Existing Conditions**
   If this is an existing rowhome, verify field dimensions, joist direction, bearing walls, party wall construction, foundation condition, roof framing, existing openings, previous alterations, moisture damage, and fire separation assemblies.

4. **Define Load Paths**
   Trace gravity loads from roof, stair, landings, guards, and floor openings down through joists, headers, trimmers, posts, walls, beams, and foundations. Identify interrupted framing and provide new members where loads are diverted.

5. **Calculate Design Loads**
   Determine dead loads, live loads, roof live/snow loads, stair live loads, guard loads, handrail loads, concentrated loads, roof garden saturated soil loads, equipment loads, drift loads, and wind uplift at curbs/parapets.

6. **Detail Stair Opening Framing**
   Size headers, trimmer joists, doubled members, hangers, posts, bearing blocks, ledger connections, and any transfer beams around the stair opening. Confirm deflection limits and vibration performance.

7. **Detail Stair and Landing Support**
   Size stair stringers, landing framing, ledgers, posts, hangers, blocking, and connections. Confirm that landings bear on real structure and that lateral restraint is provided.

8. **Check Roof Opening and Curb**
   Frame the roof hatch, stair bulkhead, or opening with proper headers, trimmers, curb support, uplift restraint, and diaphragm continuity. Coordinate curb height with roofing manufacturer requirements and expected snow/water conditions.

9. **Design Guard and Handrail Attachments**
   Detail guard posts, base plates or brackets, blocking, fasteners, embedment, edge distances, corrosion protection, and waterproofed penetrations. Check required lateral loads at top rail and infill.

10. **Coordinate Waterproofing**
    Work with the architect or roofing consultant to locate curbs, flashing, counterflashing, membranes, drainage planes, overflow paths, penetrations, and roof garden protection layers. Structural details must not compromise the roofing assembly.

11. **Coordinate Fire Separation**
    Preserve required fire-rated assemblies at party walls, stair shaft walls, ceiling/roof penetrations, and shared separations. Detail rated gypsum, shaft liner, fireblocking, draftstopping, fire-rated sealants, and protected penetrations.

12. **Verify Lateral and Diaphragm Continuity**
    Check that cutting a stair or roof opening does not break floor or roof diaphragm behavior. Add blocking, collectors, drag struts, straps, or sheathing nailing patterns where required.

13. **Check Foundations and Bearing**
    Confirm that new posts or load-bearing lines have adequate support below. If loads land on existing framing, masonry, slab, or foundation, verify capacity or design new footings/transfer framing.

14. **Produce Structural Drawings**
    Prepare plans, sections, enlarged stair/roof opening details, connection schedules, member sizes, hanger/fastener specs, guard attachment details, notes, and special inspection requirements.

15. **Coordinate With Architect and MEP**
    Resolve conflicts with headroom, roof access, insulation, drains, vents, electrical, sprinkler/fire alarm components, finishes, and egress requirements.

16. **Review Constructability**
    Confirm sequencing, temporary shoring, demolition limits, access constraints, material availability, tolerances, and whether existing framing must be sistered, replaced, or reinforced.

17. **Issue Signed/Sealed Documents**
    Finalize calculations and drawings, address permit comments, and issue signed and sealed structural documents as required by the jurisdiction.

18. **Perform Construction Administration**
    Review submittals, respond to RFIs, inspect exposed existing conditions, verify installed framing/connections before concealment, and document any field changes.
