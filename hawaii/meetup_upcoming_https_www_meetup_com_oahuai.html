About us

Exploring the real-world applications of Artificial Intelligence on O'ahu — from Pi rigs to quantized LLM agents — and trade hard-won lessons from the field.

Claude Code / Codex 101

Fri, Mar 27 · 6:00 PM HST
McCully Public Library, 2211 S. King Street, Honolulu, HI, US

Claude Code / Codex 101

By the end of this workshop you’ll complete three shipped exercises:

  1. Personal portfolio website (create + maintain + deploy)
    You’ll generate a clean site, edit it safely, push to GitHub, and deploy to a live URL (optional: custom domain + HTTPS).
  2. Landing page + signup funnel (real business asset)
    Build a simple landing page for an idea (event, service, product) with:
  • clear sections (value prop, FAQ, pricing/CTA)
  • a contact/signup form
  • deployed live via GitHub → Render
    (This is the fastest “I can actually use this tomorrow” win.)
  1. A tiny “Operator” web app (input → useful output)
    Build a small web app with a form + database that does one job extremely well—examples:
  • notes → structured brief
  • intake → task list + email draft
  • meeting → decisions + next steps
    You’ll ship it as a real app with a URL you can share.

We’ll use a requirements-driven flow: define the job, define “done,” lock constraints—and land a deployment-ready web app you can actually use and share.

Cost: $100

What you need:

  • A laptop
  • Willingness to follow along and run commands (paint-by-number style)
  • Optional: a real workflow/problem you want the app to handle (there will be time reserved to run your own projects!)

Workflow Examples:

  • content generation for marketing, branding, story telling, etc.
  • report and brief generation
  • chatbot generation & management
  • camera stream curation, transformation
  • much and many more (and you can bring your own!)

Schedule:

  • 6pm-8pm: We’ll get everyone running locally, then do a few tight iterations of the loop (requirements → plan → build → run → improve) until you’ve shipped a deployment-ready baseline. Repeat for each project.
  • 8pm-9pm (choose-your-own): Apply the same template to your workflow/problem. I’ll help you adapt the app(s) to your “X” and get you pointed at a clean next step (and optionally deploy if you’re not already live).

Location

  • Future Builders: Leading Hawai'i's Tech Renaissance

    Sat, May 16 · 10:00 AM HST
    Entrepreneurs Sandbox, 643 Ilalo St, Honolulu, HI, US

    Did you know that Hawai'i has a rich youth robotics scene?

    When Hawaiʻi’s robotics teams compete at the world championships, they’re not just building robots—they’re building the skills our islands need. Join us for an afternoon celebrating their achievements and connecting students, job seekers, career-changers, and local companies investing in Hawaiʻi’s tech future. With robot demos!

    ---

    10am Tech Job Fair
    Connect with Hawai'i companies hiring in software, clean energy, construction tech, and more.

    11:30am Pupus by Poke for the People
    Musubi, ahi rolls and vegan options from the legendary Poke for the People.

    12:30pm Youth Robotics Showcase
    Watch Hawai'i's VEX IQ teams demonstrate their championship robots. See the problem-solving, collaboration, and technical skill that took them from Honolulu garages to competing against 800+ teams worldwide.

    1:30pm Panel Discussion
    Q&A Style Panel for youth robotics leaders, tech mentors and robotics entrepreneurs.

    2:15pm Mahalos & Goodbyes
    Beach time!

    ---

    Our showcase features local robotics startups, teams from Island Robotics and coaches like Jared Haiola, who has guided dozens of Hale'iwa students to VEX IQ World Championships over the past decade. His students don't just learn robotics—they learn to solve problems with limited resources, collaborate under pressure, and build things that work.

    Free Admission For Families & Individuals
    Bring your kids. Let them see what's possible when Hawai'i's youth get the right tools and mentorship. Let them talk to the teams. Let them imagine themselves building the technology our islands need.
