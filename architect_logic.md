# Architect Logic

The following is a professional sequence a licensed architect would typically follow to detail the rowhome stair, roof access, guards, waterproofing, fire separation, accessibility, and coordination scope.

1. **Establish Project and Code Basis**
   Confirm occupancy, construction type, zoning constraints, applicable building code, local amendments, fire code, energy code, accessibility requirements, historic district rules if applicable, and rowhome-specific party-wall conditions.

2. **Document Existing Conditions**
   Survey the rowhome layout, stair location, floor-to-floor heights, roof height, parapets, drainage, exterior walls, party walls, openings, existing finishes, rated assemblies, and areas requiring selective demolition.

3. **Confirm Egress Strategy**
   Verify stair width, headroom, riser and tread geometry, landings, handrails, guards, door swings, roof access path, travel distances, emergency escape and rescue openings where applicable, and any required separation from occupied spaces.

4. **Coordinate Stair Geometry**
   Lay out the stair in plan and section so every run, landing, and transition clears required headroom. Coordinate the stair opening with floor framing, roof framing, finishes, guard locations, and usable circulation space.

5. **Define Roof Access Condition**
   Determine whether roof access is through a hatch, bulkhead, penthouse, or open stair condition. Confirm weather protection, guard requirements, parapet height, door or hatch operation, landing clearances, and roof maintenance access.

6. **Detail Roof Opening**
   Draw enlarged plans and sections through the roof opening, curb, parapet or bulkhead, insulation transitions, air barrier continuity, vapor control, flashing, counterflashing, and roof membrane terminations.

7. **Coordinate Waterproofing**
   Specify roof membrane type, curb heights, slope to drain, scuppers or internal drains, overflow drainage, protection board, walk pads, roof garden layers, root barrier, drainage mat, filter fabric, and waterproofing at guard or rail penetrations.

8. **Preserve Fire Separation**
   Identify required fire-rated walls, floors, ceilings, party walls, stair shaft enclosures, penetrations, and concealed spaces. Coordinate rated gypsum, shaft assemblies, fireblocking, draftstopping, firestopping, rated doors, and protected membrane penetrations.

9. **Coordinate Structural Scope**
   Provide the structural engineer with stair opening dimensions, roof access geometry, guard locations, roof garden loads, curb locations, framing conflicts, and penetrations. Incorporate structural member sizes and connection requirements into the architectural drawings.

10. **Detail Guards and Handrails**
    Confirm guard height, handrail height, graspability, extensions, post spacing, opening limitations, attachment locations, corrosion resistance, and waterproofing at all roof, stair, and landing conditions.

11. **Coordinate Envelope and Energy Performance**
    Maintain continuous insulation, air barrier, water-resistive barrier, vapor control, and thermal continuity at the stair enclosure, roof opening, curbs, parapets, and party-wall interfaces.

12. **Resolve Interior Finish Transitions**
    Detail stair finish build-ups, nosings, wall finishes, base conditions, thresholds, ceiling planes, access panels, lighting locations, smoke/CO detection, and finish continuity around rated assemblies.

13. **Coordinate MEP and Life Safety**
    Coordinate lighting, switching, emergency lighting where required, sprinklers if present, alarms, ventilation, roof drains, vents, conduits, ductwork, and penetrations through rated or waterproofed assemblies.

14. **Prepare Permit Documents**
    Produce code analysis, life-safety plans, demolition plans, floor plans, roof plans, reflected ceiling plans as needed, wall sections, stair sections, enlarged details, schedules, specifications, and coordinated notes.

15. **Review Constructability**
    Confirm demolition sequencing, temporary weather protection, stair installation access, roof membrane sequencing, protection of existing finishes, tolerances, field verification requirements, and coordination between trades.

16. **Coordinate With Authorities Having Jurisdiction**
    Submit permit documents, respond to plan review comments, clarify code interpretations, coordinate inspections, and revise drawings as required by the jurisdiction.

17. **Administer Construction**
    Review submittals, shop drawings, product data, waterproofing details, guardrail details, rated assembly documentation, and contractor RFIs. Visit the site at key milestones to observe general conformance with the contract documents.

18. **Document Closeout**
    Collect warranties, roof membrane documentation, firestopping records, inspection approvals, as-built updates, maintenance instructions, and any owner requirements for roof access, drainage maintenance, and guard/handrail upkeep.
